Instructions for Using this Rubric

Before you write your resume, collect all your employment history. Check your resume against this rubric while completing the checklist.

The Rubric

Criteria

Does not meet specifications

Meets specifications

Exceeds specifications

(Completely Udacious)

General

  • Resume is longer than one page
  • Resume is not in PDF format
  • Links are not hyperlinked
  • There are spelling or grammatical errors
  • Resume includes one of (and should not): age, gender, marital status, photo, an objective statement or mention of references.
  • Resume uses language or acronyms that are unknown to employers (and should not)
  • Resume includes negative language (and should not)
  • Resume does not use a standard order: eg. Heading, Skills, Projects, Relevant Experience, Education.
  • Content in subsections is not listed reverse chronologically

  • Resume fits on one page
  • Resume is in PDF format
  • Links are hyperlinked
  • Spelling and grammar are correct
  • Resume does not include: age, gender, marital status, photo, an objective statement or mention of references.
  • Resume avoids language or acronyms that are unknown to employers
  • Resume does not include negative language
  • Resume uses a standard order: eg. Heading, Skills, Projects, Relevant Experience, Education.
  • Content in subsections is listed reverse chronologically

  • Resume has link to professional twitter account, professional blog or online portfolio.

Heading

  • Heading does not include all of: full name, phone number, email, LinkedIn and Github profiles (if relevant).

  • Heading includes: full name, phone number, email, LinkedIn and Github profiles (if relevant).

Skills

  • Relevant and challenging technologies are not listed.
  • If resume displays a wide range of skills, has not been emphasized.

  • Relevant and challenging technologies are listed: programming languages, frameworks, IDEs, version control systems, databases, operating systems, image/video/audio editing software.
  • If resume displays a wide range of skills, student notes which skills are of particular expertise.

Education

  • Nanodegree is not included
  • College, major and honors are not listed, or are poorly formatted.
  • Courses are listed which are not directly applicable
  • GPA listed when not first job out or college or not above 3.5.
  • Education section is misplaced
  • Nanodegree included (note “in progress” if needed).
  • Any college, major and honors are included.
  • If particularly applicable, relevant courses are listed (especially if outside major).
  • GPA is listed only if first job out of college or above 3.5.
  • Education section is at top if it is the first job out of college OR degree is from a prestigious institution.

  • If prestigious institution attended, education section moved to top

Projects & Experience

Projects

  • Irrelevant (to the job) projects are listed
  • Projects descriptions do not mention the important technologies used to create them

Relevant Experience

  • Experiences do not list company/ organization, title of role, start and end date (month & year), location, title
  • Irrelevant (to the job) experience is listed

Projects

  • Only most relevant projects are listed, but many types are acceptable
  • Technologies used to create projects have are in list format (possibly with bullet points) and have descriptions

Relevant Experience

  • Experiences note company/ organization, title of role, start and end date (month & year), location, title
  • Relevant experience listed, including unpaid or part-time. Omitted the words “part-time” and “unpaid”

Projects

  • Open source contributions are highlighted

Other Honors

  • Awards, publications, presentations are correct and correctly formatted.

Bullet Points

  • More than 4 bullet points are used
  • Sub-bullet points are used
  • Bullet points do not start with action verbs
  • Incorrect tense is used
  • Bullet points do not note specifically how you benefited your company or cause
  • Bullet points lack concrete, numerical evidence
  • Bullet points are longer than one sentence and/or longer than one and a half lines

  • Maximum 3-4 bullet points
  • No sub-bullet points
  • Bullet points start with action verbs
  • Correct tense is used in bullet points: past tense for previous, current tense for on-going
  • Bullet points note specifically how you benefited your company or cause
  • Bullet points provide concrete, numerical evidence, eg. projects completed, money made, or people managed, accomplishments (eg. % increase)
  • Bullet points are one sentence maximum, not longer than one and a half lines

Targeted for Profession

Switching Careers

  • Does not include a 2-3 sentence career summary (or needs to be moved near the top) explaining the switch with evidence of interest and ability in new field.

Resume Target for Job

  • Keywords used are not appropriate for field
  • Resume is not customized for specific company
  • Resume uses an insufficient variety of key skills, traits and responsibilities highlighted, specific to the job.
  • Skills and traits listed are not qualified with concrete proof of these skills or traits.
  • Some experiences listed do not help prove success in job’s responsibilities

Interests, Hobbies (optional)

  • Interests and hobbies are included which may be inappropriate or take up a disproportionate amount of space on the resume.

Switching Careers

  • Includes a 2-3 sentence career summary (placed near the top of your resume) explaining the switch with evidence of interest and ability in new field.

Resume Target for Job

  • Appropriate keywords for your field are used
  • Resume is customized for specific company
  • Resume uses a sufficient variety of key skills, traits and responsibilities highlighted, specific to the job.
  • Resume proves you have skills and traits specific to the job.
  • Experiences are selected to prove success in job’s responsibilities.

Interests, Hobbies (optional)

  • A concise list of appropriate interests and hobbies are included

Style

  • Text is not aligned to left or right axis
  • Company name and title are not emphasized
  • Project name and role are not emphasized
  • Content is not optimized for horizontal space
  • Lists of single words are not condensed into the same line
  • Section headings are not distinct from other text
  • Name is not at the top of the page or not larger than other text.
  • Section formatting is inconsistent: bullet points, section headings, work history

  • All text is aligned to left or right axis
  • Company name and title are emphasized/distinct (includes using columns for company, title, date)
  • Project name and role are emphasized
  • Content optimized for horizontal space
  • Lists of single words are condensed into the same line
  • Section headings distinct from other text
  • Name is at the top of the page and larger than other text.
  • Section formatting is consistent: bullet points, section headings, work history

  • Styling of name and basic information has a personal flair, yet is professional (styling is within suggested size limits)
